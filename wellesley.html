Town of Wellesley Engineering Division

Mechanical and construction engineering co-op work involving CAD drafting, field data collection, digital inspection workflows, and Python-based engineering automation.

At a glance

This role combined office work, field work, engineering communication, and systems improvement. I supported the engineering division by creating drawings, improving digital processes, collecting field measurements, and helping convert older workflows into more reliable and efficient ones.

What I worked on

My work centered on improving how the engineering division collected, organized, and used information. I created full AutoCAD plan sets, brought field measurements into drafting workflows, helped update sewer, drainage, and utility plans, and built digital systems that made inspections and documentation more efficient.

Digital inspection workflow

One of the biggest improvements I worked on was helping move the department away from paper-based inspection forms. I designed and implemented a workflow using ArcGIS Online, Survey123, and Field Maps so inspectors could enter information digitally in the field and make that information immediately available to engineers back in the office.

This improved data accuracy, reduced turnaround time, and created a more organized process that the department could continue using beyond my co-op term.

Python automation

I wrote several Python programs to reduce repetitive manual work. One of the most useful automation efforts involved converting hand-drawn field notes and sketches into CAD-ready vectors. I also created scripts to improve organization and reduce time spent on repetitive file and drafting tasks.

The broader result was that the office could spend less time on repetitive steps and more time on engineering work that actually required review and decision-making.

CAD drafting and plan development

I independently created multiple AutoCAD proposals and plan sets, starting from field conditions and existing measurements and carrying those drawings through proposal and final plan stages. I worked on existing conditions, proposals, and final construction plan documents used by the engineering division.

This gave me strong experience translating physical site conditions into clear, usable engineering drawings.

Field data collection

I operated total station and Trimble GPS equipment to collect survey and topographic information in the field. I then brought that data back into CAD and GIS workflows to support design and planning work. That made the role especially valuable because I was seeing both sides of the engineering process: how data is captured and how that data becomes design information.
