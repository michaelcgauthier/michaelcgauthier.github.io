Major workstreams (portfolio-style, not resume-style).

Digital Inspection Workflow (ArcGIS Online / Survey123 / Field Maps)

What: The department relied on paper inspection forms, which slowed down turnaround and made data hard to share.

How: Built the inspection workflow online from end to end (forms + field map setup + consistent structure so inspections were recorded the same way).

Result: Inspections became faster to complete and easier to track, and engineers could access field data immediately without waiting for paper to be re-entered.

Drafting Automation (Python → CAD/GIS Linework)

What: Drafting from hand sketches/field notes took significant time and created inconsistencies depending on who drew it.

How: Wrote Python programs to convert hand-drawn lines/text into vector geometry so multiple drawings could be processed in batches.

Result: Reduced repetitive manual drafting work and made it easier to standardize how information was brought into CAD/GIS.

Field-to-CAD Workflow (Survey Equipment → Plan Development)

What: Projects required accurate site measurements that could be used directly for design and documentation.

How: Collected survey/topographic data with Trimble GPS and total station equipment, then integrated it into CAD/GIS for plan work.

Result: Faster turnaround from field measurement to drawings and fewer gaps between site conditions and what was shown in plans.

Utility / Drainage / Sewer Plan Updates + Conflict Flagging

What: Field conditions regularly differed from drawings, and conflicts needed to be caught before they caused rework.

How: Updated sewer/drainage/utility plans based on site observations and flagged conflicts early during review and inspection cycles.

Result: Helped reduce on-site rework by catching issues earlier in the process.
